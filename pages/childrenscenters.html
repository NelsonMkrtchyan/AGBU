Let the Space Fit the Dream

School is for learning about the world around us. Afterschool is for developing one’s character and self-purpose within. That’s why, after a quarter-century of success, AGBU is looking to keep its three Children’s Centers aligned with the future by funding a major overhaul of the interior spaces.

The Afterschool Reimagined Campaign is a $12 million facilities renewal initiative designed to ensure that no child is turned away from our children’s centers due to lack of capacity or resources. Within a timeframe of 3-4 years, our goal is to double student enrollment from "1,700" students currently to 4,000 across all three centers. These upgrades will also create the right conditions for expanding curricula and enhancing teaching tools.

Facilities Reimagined

Each of the centers will share the same aesthetic sensibility and design philosophy:

  • Green eco-friendly construction materials
  • Open workspaces, natural lighting and versatile furnishings
  • Updated equipment, supplies, public safety and hygiene standards
  • Dedicated spaces for family and community events
  • A renovated Senior Dining Center facility housed on the premises
Green spaces for eco-friendly and outdoor usage
Optimized capacity, for maximum creativity and comfort
Open and inviting classrooms

Education and Management Reimagined


To empower teachers and management to perform and lead at the highest standards of excellence, we will institute:

  • Best practices teaching methods and intensive workshops
  • New pedagogy that encourages critical thinking, teamwork and self-discipline
  • Strategic partnerships and exchanges
  • Improved communications among administrators, teachers and parents
  • Adjusted salaries based on merit and hours served
  • Increased faculty to accommodate a larger student body
  • An advisory board and management to oversee excellence in the arts and oversee strategy and curriculum

Community Outreach Reimagined

To help uplift the communities we serve, this initiative will also benefit:

  • Family members: Renovated waiting and reception areas will facilitate community interaction.
  • Senior citizens: AGBU Senior Dining Centers, which are housed in the three Children’s Centers, will be upgraded to provide a more inviting, convenient and comfortable interior for the 600+ senior guests
