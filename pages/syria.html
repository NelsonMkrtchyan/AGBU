THE WOMEN OF W.E.

There’s an ample supply of talented, capable women who are ready, willing, and eager to start or expand a micro-enterprise or home-business.
Here are five of the 75 women already benefiting from the support and services of AGBU W.E.

ARPINE HAMBARDZUMYAN

Location: Yerevan

Business: Custom-made shoes

“I was playing outside. A mortar shell burst out of nowhere ripping open my left leg. Suddenly, I was a teenager bound to a wheelchair. I stayed home because I didn’t want people to stare at my wound or pity me. I lost many friends that way. My parents couldn’t afford a prosthetic leg. Then some caring Armenians from far away read my story in the AGBU magazine. They collected the money to pay for it. The best way I can thank them is to do good things in my life. And always stand tall as a proud Armenian.”

SHUSHAN AVETISYAN

Location: Yeghegnadzor, Vayots Dzor

Business: Lavash and pastry production

“A space for Syrian Armenian relief operations was donated by AGBU’s Vahe Karapetian Center. AGBU distributes its donations regularly through this office. For over seven years we have been trying to heal the many wounds. As a fellow refugee from Syria myself, I well understand that there are many who had considerable wealth in Syria, both moral and material, and lost it in a few hours. People accustomed to a certain quality of life suddenly have to find work and face social problems. Many of these refugees fit the category of highly vulnerable.”

ANIK ASATRYAN

Location: Zangakatun, Ararat

Business: Dried fruits production

“One day a little girl in class said, ‘I need to talk today.’ Then they all started talking about what they had been through. I was their teacher but I acted as a mom and psychologist, too. If their parents weren’t comfortable, the children weren’t either. So, the families were taken care of, completely. I love the support our school lends, that AGBU lends.”
